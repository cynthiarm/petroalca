Strategic Operation Points & Logistics Routes

Petroalca’s logistics network ensures safe, efficient, and on-time fuel transportation between U.S.–Mexico border terminals and national distribution corridors.

Strategic Operation Points
  • Harlingen, TX: 500,000-barrel terminal located 26 km from Los Indios Bridge.
  • Los Indios, TX: 125-hectare site for a future exclusive export terminal.
  • Pharr / Reynosa: Border crossing with national logistics connectivity.
  • Brownsville / Matamoros (Los Tomates): Corridor with overweight route access.
Operational Routes
  • Harlingen Port → Los Indios Bridge (15 min)
  • Harlingen → Pharr / Reynosa (45 min)
  • Brownsville → Los Tomates (30 min)

These routes ensure fast, reliable, cost-efficient cross-border supply chain operations.

Our Terminal — Harlingen, Texas

Strategically located near the U.S.–Mexico border, our terminal ensures continuous, compliant, and high-volume fuel supply operations.

The Harlingen Terminal connects directly to key logistics routes and ports, offering scalable capacity for gasoline and ULSD diesel distribution. With advanced storage infrastructure and efficient multimodal access, it is a vital hub for energy operations between the U.S. and Mexico.

Own Barges

Our fleet of dedicated barges guarantees reliable and cost-effective transportation across inland waterways, ensuring fuel continuity and efficiency.

Our barge operations provide flexible, high-capacity transport to complement land-based logistics. Combined with our terminals, they strengthen Petroalca’s position as a reliable link between U.S. refineries and Mexican markets.

Loading and Storage

Efficient Loading and Unloading Operations.

Petroalca’s terminals are equipped with modern pumping systems, metering technology, and secure handling zones that streamline every transfer operation. From barge discharge to truck loading, each step is monitored to ensure product integrity, operational safety, and timely movement across the supply chain.
